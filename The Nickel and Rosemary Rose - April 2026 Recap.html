What moved the month

Forecast risks & watch-items

Three things to monitor

Volume vs rate trade-off. May/Jun/Jul forecast ADRs ($520 / $438 / $365) all sit below April's $582 actual. If the rate discipline can hold above forecast even by $30–50, the upside is material — May forecast NI of +$116K becomes +$150K+.

July softness. Forecast Net Income of -$141.7K in July is the inverse of April. Charleston heat plus reduced corporate-travel demand. Need a confident operational plan for July labor scaling or contribution margin compresses sharply.

F&B labor scaling. Forecast F&B expense lines are at budgeted prime-cost levels — the +$26.8K April labor win is not yet built into the May-Jul lines. Holding the April labor leverage through the peak is a $50–80K upside.

Section 3 · Takeaways

May is where the forecast gets validated.

Strengths

May Net Income +$116.3K forecast — second consecutive profitable month if achieved. Three-month NOI of $1.05M. April ADR cleared all three forecast ADRs — rate runway exists.

Watchout

July Net Income -$141.7K — single-month dip into the red. June forecast ADR of $438 is the lowest mid-quarter rate; verify pickup pace weekly through May. Forecast file labels Jun/Jul as Budget (not Forecast).

Opportunity

Holding April's rate strength through May would lift Net Income materially. Carrying the F&B Payroll discipline (-$26.8K April) forward as a structural change is worth $50–80K through the May–Jul window.

Next step

Lock the May pickup pace review with revenue management. Build the July operational plan (labor scaling, demand stimulation) ahead of June 15. Convert May to a formal rolling forecast vs the underlying budget posture.

Q2/Q3 2026 Priorities · Flagged for review

Six priorities. Three for the hotel. Three for Rosemary Rose.

April closed ahead of plan on rate, NOI, sentiment, and rate index — and crossed the first standalone-profitable month. The Q2/Q3 priorities frame the work to defend the rate leadership (ARI 111 needs to travel into May/June even as forecast ADRs compress), close the residual occupancy gap (Sun/Mon MPI 72/73), and convert April's outlet labor leverage into a structural cost-base change through the summer ramp. All priorities flagged for owner/operator review before committing to ownership reporting.

Hotel · Priority 1 · Flagged for review

Defend the ARI 110 rate position.

April cleared ARI 110.7 — highest of the inaugural year, #2 of 7 ADR rank. May/Jun/Jul forecast ADRs are $30–230 below April actual. The work is sustaining rate above the rolling forecast — every $30 of ADR above plan = ~$15–20 RevPAR upside on stabilized occupancy. No OTA-NREF discount reflex.

Metric: ARI ≥ 105 May, ≥ 100 Jun/Jul. Owner: Revenue Management

Hotel · Priority 2 · Flagged for review

Close the Sun–Mon MPI gap.

April's Tuesday MPI lifted from 73 (Q1) to 90 — the weekday-build playbook worked. Sun MPI is now 72 / Mon MPI 73 — the new weekday-build target. Lever: Sun-night extended-stay packaging, midweek-3-night offers, off-peak rate fences.

Metric: Sun MPI ≥ 80 by June, Mon MPI ≥ 80 by July. Owner: Revenue Management + Marketing

Hotel · Priority 3 · Flagged for review

Trend Other Non-Op against $0 budget.

YTD-through-April Other Non-Op is $182.5K against a $0 verified budget. Same intentional-zero pattern as Q1. Trend through May to baseline run-rate; ownership should set a non-budget expectation for the line by June so the variance reporting reads cleanly.

Metric: Confirmed monthly run-rate by 6/30. Owner: Finance + Ownership

Rosemary Rose · Priority 1 · Flagged for review

Convert April labor leverage to structural.

April F&B Payroll came in $26.8K under plan — prime cost 48.5% vs 66.1% plan. The forecast hasn't picked up this discipline. The work is making the April scheduling pattern (lower labor-per-cover) the May/Jun baseline, not a one-off. $20–30K of monthly contribution upside if sustained.

Metric: Labor % of F&B Revenue ≤ 30% in May/Jun. Owner: Rosemary Rose GM

Rosemary Rose · Priority 2 · Flagged for review

Plan the July step-down.

July forecast F&B contribution margin drops to 33% (vs 42% May/Jun). The risk is that labor doesn't scale down with covers — locking in a July scheduling plan by June 15 protects contribution. The April win can be lost in one month of over-staffing.

Metric: July labor plan filed by 6/15. Cadence: Weekly review. Owner: GM + Finance

Rosemary Rose · Priority 3 · Flagged for review

Stand up Food/Bev cost reporting.

April month F&B revenue mix split (Food 16% / Bev 80%) is available from Toast, but Food Cost % and Beverage Cost % require monthly invoice detail — not provided in April source files for this build. Q1 had this detail. The work is restoring the monthly food/bev cost cadence before May closes.

Metric: Monthly Food/Bev Cost % filed by 6/10 (May close). Owner: GM + Finance
Risks & Opportunities · Flagged for review

Forward-looking summary across both businesses.

Risks

What could compress the forecast

  • Rate flinch under forecast pressure. May forecast ADR is $61 below April actual. The temptation to add inventory at OTA-NREF rate codes to "hit forecast occupancy" erodes the ARI 111 position. Each 5-point ARI loss is ~$25 RevPAR.
  • F&B labor slippage. April's 48.5% prime cost is the win. Without explicit scheduling discipline, May/Jun could revert to the budget assumption (~57%). $20–30K monthly drag if it slips.
  • Other Non-Op acceleration. $182.5K YTD against $0 budget. Cash Flow Before Debt is being compressed by ~$45K/month — set the ownership expectation explicitly.
  • July contribution drop. Forecast July Net Income -$141.7K. Single-month dip back into the red. Operating plan needs to be filed before June 30.
  • Sun/Mon MPI sticking at 72. Without packaging launch by mid-May, Sun-night MPI won't close — RGI plateaus.
  • Location-category sentiment dip. 3.0 (-0.4 vs March) on small mention base. If May shows the same pattern, the signal is real — operational diagnosis needed.

Opportunities

Where May/Jul can outperform forecast

  • ARI 115 stretch. April hit 111. May at 115 would add ~$30K of contribution. Demand-peak validation suggests this is reachable.
  • F&B prime cost held at 48%. Sustaining April's labor leverage through May/Jun = $40–60K of F&B contribution above forecast.
  • Sun/Mon packaging. Closing the 8-point Sun MPI gap = ~$20 RevPAR / ~$30K monthly revenue. Compounds direct-channel mix.
  • Direct channel mix continued growth. April direct retail at 59.9% (vs 56% Q1). Lifting to 65% in summer saves $25–35K in OTA commissions.
  • Penthouse pricing. April $1,500+ penthouse ADR validates the rate ceiling. June bookings priced ahead of the trajectory.
  • Sentiment as marketing asset. Apr 4.89 / 100% Recommended / 100% response coverage is direct-booking creative. Lifting direct channel mix is the conversion.
Closing Read

April was the breakout month. May is the test.

The Nickel cleared April with revenue ahead of plan ($1.07M vs $932K), NOI ahead of plan (+36%), ADR rank moving to #2 of 7 in the Charleston comp set, Survey NPS at 82, and the property's first standalone-profitable month of the inaugural year (+$101.5K Cash Flow After Debt). Rosemary Rose ran F&B contribution margin at 46.3% on a 1,760-bps favorable prime-cost swing — the cleanest outlet read of the inaugural year. The Q2/Q3 work, framed in Section 10, is about converting April's wins from event into baseline: rate strength sustained into May/Jun, labor leverage made structural, and the residual Sun/Mon occupancy gap closed by midsummer. If May lands at or ahead of the forecast NOI of $471K, the property will have crossed Cash Flow After Debt for the second time in a row — and the ramp pattern shifts from "inaugural-year build" to "stabilized luxury." That's the read.
